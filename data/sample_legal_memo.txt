Client: Jane Doe
Matter: Personal Injury Claim

Facts:
Jane Doe was rear-ended at a stoplight on January 12, 2025. She reported neck pain, headaches, and reduced range of motion within two days of the incident. Medical imaging did not show fracture, but physical therapy was recommended.

Issue:
Whether the medical records and treatment timeline support a claim for continuing injury and damages.

Analysis:
The records indicate prompt reporting of symptoms and continuity of treatment. Follow-up visits document persistent pain and work limitations. These facts support causation and damages, though the absence of fracture may be raised by the defense. The treatment history and physician notes remain important evidence.

Conclusion:
The documents support a plausible claim for ongoing injury, especially if treatment continuity and physician assessments are emphasized.
